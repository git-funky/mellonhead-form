Mellonhead Labs

Three Labs, each built for a specific audience and outcome.

Leaders Lab (3 sessions)

For executives and senior leaders. Develop calibrated judgment about what today's AI can really do and where it falls short. Learn a repeatable framework for finding and prioritizing AI opportunities, applied first to your own function then across the business. Walk out with a real decision you're ready to act on, with a change-leadership lens to navigate it.

People Managers Lab (5 sessions)

For managers shaping how their teams work. Find AI opportunities in your team's workflows, then decide whether you're optimizing what already exists or redesigning how the work gets done. Define readiness criteria for safe rollout, and lead your team through the change.

Builders Labs (topic-based)

For team members building with AI. Focused short Labs that go deep on one skill at a time: building Copilot agents, building internal tools with AI, evaluating AI outputs, applying design thinking to AI work, and more. Each Lab ends with a working artifact participants built themselves.

How a Lab works

Training alone doesn't build AI capability. People build it by working on their actual problems, with someone to help when they get stuck.

Every Lab is built around three things:

Real work, not exercises. Participants work on actual projects sitting in their queue. The artifact at the end of a Lab is something they'll use Monday morning.

Paired coaching, not lectures. Each participant gets 1:1 advisory time alongside the cohort sessions. That's where the messy questions get answered and the work actually gets shaped.

Async feedback between sessions. Participants apply what they learn to real work between sessions, bring it back for feedback, and refine. The skill builds with the work, not separately from it.

It's "do it with us." Deep enough to shift how the work gets done, light enough that your team carries the capability forward after we're gone.

You walk away with a working agent, skill, or workflow. And the AI capability to build the next one.

AI Curriculum for Everyone

Foundational AI training customized to your industry, policies, and AI tools. Two tracks:

Foundations. Core courses include AI fundamentals, a Microsoft 365 Copilot course (five apps, 2–3 useful tasks per app), and a customized AI policy course built to your organization's guidelines (the "AI @ Your Org" course). An optional Judgment add-on includes two scenario-based courses: AI Ethics and Maintaining Trust.

Skills. Seven blended-learning courses on the AI skills that come up across roles: communicating with AI, brainstorming, extracting data, summarizing, working with data, building presentations, and creating documentation. Each pairs a short self-paced intro with a 90-minute hands-on practice session.

These two work well together. Curriculum builds the baseline literacy. A Lab goes deep where it matters most.

What an engagement looks like

Engagements typically run 3 to 6 months and combine one or more offers. Cadence varies: weekly when teams need fast skill-building, monthly when leaders need integration time between sessions. Every Lab includes 1:1 advisory and async feedback in addition to live cohort time.

Sample engagement shapes

Leadership readiness for a small executive team. The Leaders Lab over 3 months, one session per month. 8 to 12 executives. Each leader walks out with a real decision they're acting on by the end of the program, and a change-leadership framework for the next one.

Manager-led rollout for a 200-person organization. Foundations curriculum is customized with your policies and industry context over 4 to 6 weeks, then rolls out across all teams to build baseline AI literacy. A People Managers Lab runs in parallel, taking the managers through the 5-session arc over two months. Each manager walks out with a defined workflow change they're piloting with their team.

Builders cohort building Copilot agents. Two in-person half-day sessions in one week: requirements first, then buildout. A virtual launch-planning session follows four weeks later, drawing on test results and limitations identified between sessions. Weekly office hours and async check-ins support builders throughout. Up to 24 participants. Each launches an agent they built and tested themselves.
